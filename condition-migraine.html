What this page is for

Use this page to prepare for a conversation with a GP surgery, pharmacist, NHS 111, an existing specialist team or a relevant support organisation. It is designed to help you explain what has changed and what support you need.

Emergency safety: if symptoms could be life-threatening, call 999 or go to A&E. If you need medical help now and are unsure what to do, use NHS 111 online or call 111.

Prepare before asking for help

  • Write down when symptoms started and whether they are improving, worsening or changing.
  • Record frequency, severity, triggers and how symptoms affect sleep, work, school, eating, walking or daily life.
  • List medicines, doses, missed doses, allergies, side effects and any recent changes.
  • Bring relevant test results, appointment letters, discharge summaries or clinic contact details.
  • Decide the main question you want answered before the appointment ends.

Questions to ask

  • What is the most appropriate next step?
  • Do I need tests, monitoring, a medication review or a referral?
  • What should I do if symptoms get worse while I am waiting?
  • Who should I contact for follow-up, and when should I expect a reply?
  • Are there trusted written resources I should read after this conversation?
Useful next step: create clear notes with the appointment planner or print a symptom diary to show patterns over time.
