Where to get medical help in the UK

A navigation guide to common UK routes. It helps you choose who to contact, but it does not diagnose symptoms or replace professional advice.

1. Life-threatening emergency

Call 999 or go to A&E if you think there is an immediate life-threatening emergency.

2. Need medical help now but unsure?

Use NHS 111 online or call 111. NHS 111 can direct people to the most suitable service.

3. Ongoing symptoms or long-term condition

Your GP surgery is usually the starting point for ongoing symptoms, long-term conditions, medication reviews and non-emergency referrals.

4. Minor illness or medicine questions

A pharmacist can help with many common minor illnesses and medicine questions. They may also advise when to contact a GP or NHS 111.

5. Existing specialist team

If you already have a specialist team, such as an IBD nurse or hospital clinic, check your letters or patient portal for the best contact route.
